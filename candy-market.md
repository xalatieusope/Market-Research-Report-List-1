<p><p>The candy market is primarily divided into two main types: hard candy and soft candy. Hard candy is characterized by its firm texture and long shelf life, often made by boiling sugar and flavored extracts, resulting in varieties like lollipops and candy canes. In contrast, soft candy offers a chewy or creamy consistency, encompassing gummies, marshmallows, and nougats. Each type caters to different consumer preferences, with hard candy typically appealing to those seeking long-lasting flavor, while soft candy attracts those enjoying a more indulgent texture.</p></p>
