<p><p>The cotton pulp market is divided into two main types: bleached and unbleached cotton pulp. Bleached cotton pulp undergoes a chemical process to remove natural color and impurities, resulting in a bright white product that is highly sought after for high-quality applications such as specialty paper and food packaging. In contrast, unbleached cotton pulp retains its natural color and fibers, offering a more eco-friendly option often used in products where color is less critical, like cardboard and industrial applications.</p></p>
