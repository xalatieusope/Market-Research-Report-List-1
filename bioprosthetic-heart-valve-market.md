<p><ul><li>Porcine Aortic Tissue Valve</li><li>Bovine Pericardial Tissue Valve</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bioprosthetic heart valve market primarily features two types: porcine aortic tissue valves and bovine pericardial tissue valves. Porcine aortic tissue valves utilize tissues from pigs and are valued for their durability and natural compatibility with the human body. Bovine pericardial tissue valves, sourced from cattle, offer similar benefits but are often noted for their flexibility and resistance to calcification. Both types are used in patients requiring valve replacement, providing alternatives to mechanical valves with less risk of thrombosis.</p></p>
