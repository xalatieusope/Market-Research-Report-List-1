<p><p>Infrared sensors are categorized into two main types: thermal and quantum. Thermal infrared sensors detect heat emitted by objects, converting thermal radiation into measurable signals, making them ideal for applications like surveillance and temperature measurement. Quantum infrared sensors, on the other hand, rely on quantum phenomena to detect infrared light and are highly sensitive, enabling precise measurements. Each type serves distinct applications across industries such as automotive, aerospace, and medical, driven by their unique detection mechanisms and sensitivity levels.</p></p>
