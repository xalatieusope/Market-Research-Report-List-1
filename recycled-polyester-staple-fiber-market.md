<p><p>The recycled polyester staple fiber market is segmented into solid fiber and hollow fiber types. Solid fiber is dense and often used in applications like textiles and upholstery, providing durability and stability. In contrast, hollow fiber features a tubular structure that enhances insulation and softness, making it ideal for products like bedding and outerwear. Both types contribute to sustainability by repurposing plastic waste, appealing to environmentally conscious consumers and industries seeking eco-friendly materials for their products.</p></p>
