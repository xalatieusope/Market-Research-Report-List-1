<p><p>The synthetic and bio-based polypropylene market includes three main types: isotactic polypropylene, random polypropylene, and metatactic polypropylene. Isotactic polypropylene, known for its high strength and rigidity, is commonly used in packaging and automotive applications. Random polypropylene, with its amorphous structure, offers flexibility and transparency, making it suitable for films and containers. Metatactic polypropylene, less common, exhibits unique properties beneficial for specialized applications. The growing demand for sustainable materials is driving interest in bio-based alternatives within these polypropylene types.</p></p>
